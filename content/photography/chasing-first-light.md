It was 5 AM when the storm broke. Standing at the edge of Pahalgam as the sun began to light up the frosted peaks of Kashimr, there was a profound weight of absolute silence. 

Every branch was pillowed in dry snow. In photography, we talk about framing, shadows, and contrast. But the real essence of any frame is how it crystallizes a single, ephemeral breath of time. Shot on Fuji X-T4, 35mm F/1.4, 1/500s at ISO200.
